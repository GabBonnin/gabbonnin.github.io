## Overview

The blended-learning courses DiSkO and DiSkO KiJu teach knowledge and practical skills for the evidence-based diagnosis of mental disorders in adults and in children and adolescents, respectively. The courses focus on training students in the administration of structured diagnostic interviews, using the DIPS-OA 1.2 (Margraf et al., 2021) and the Kinder-DIPS-OA (Schneider et al., 2017) as exemplary instruments.

Each course consists of nine asynchronous online lessons designed to provide theoretical foundations and practical guidance. In addition, four synchronous sessions with instructors are recommended to practice interview skills and discuss course content.

The DiSkO courses were evaluated in a multi-center study with a sample of N = 350 students and compared with traditional diagnostic training courses (Bonnin et al., 2024). The results showed that the DiSkO courses were superior to traditional courses in teaching both practical diagnostic skills and theoretical knowledge. Furthermore, students rated the DiSkO courses as at least as good as traditional courses on almost all additional evaluation dimensions, indicating strong acceptance and overall effectiveness.
