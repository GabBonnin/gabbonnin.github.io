## Overview

META investigates whether large language models (LLMs) can be used to measure psychological constructs from open-ended text written by patients at intake into psychotherapy. Unlike prior work relying on social media data, META focuses on ecological validity by working with data from an actual German outpatient psychotherapy clinic.

## Key Questions

- Can LLMs reliably measure constructs like depression severity, anxiety, and interpersonal problems from patient narratives?
- How do LLM-based assessments compare to traditional self-report measures (PHQ-9, GAD-7, OQ-45)?
- What are the psychometric properties of text-derived assessments?

## Methods

- German outpatient psychotherapy clinic data
- Open-ended intake responses processed with state-of-the-art LLMs
- Comparison with established questionnaire-based assessments
- Focus on construct validity, reliability, and clinical utility
