Major depressive disorder is a leading cause of disability worldwide, and despite evidence based treatments, many patients do not achieve remission. Personalised modular psychotherapy appears particularly promising for the treatment of treatment-resistant depression (TRD), but no confirmatory randomised trial has yet tested whether it outperforms a state-of-the-art active comparator in TRD.

POSIDONIA is a sequential, Bayesian, randomised, parallel-group trial within the German Center for Mental Health (DZPG) evaluating the superiority of personalised psychotherapy compared with Behavioral Activation. The primary endpoint is the difference in depression severity at week 6 between personalised PT and BA, measured using the Montgomery–Åsberg Depression Rating Scale.

This trial has the potential to modify existing clinical guidelines on the treatment of TRD thereby optimizing routine clinical care. In addition, POSIDONIA aims to establish a adaptive trial infrastructure across all six sites of the DZPG that can be expanded for iterative optimisation of psychotherapeutic interventions.
