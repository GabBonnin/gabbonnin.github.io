Major depressive disorder is a leading cause of disability worldwide, and despite evidence based treatments, many patients do not achieve remission. Personalised modular psychotherapy appears particularly promising for the treatment of treatment-resistant depression (TRD), but no confirmatory randomised trial has yet tested whether it outperforms a state-of-the-art active comparator in TRD.

The primary objective of POSIDONIA is to test the superiority of personalised modular psychotherapy (personalised PT) over behavioural activation (BA). Participants (N=250) will be adults aged 18–70, who meet criteria for TRD. The treatment will encompass 12 individual sessions of either personalised PT or BA over the course of 6
weeks (two 50-minute sessions per week). POSIDONIA is a sequential, Bayesian, randomised, parallel-group trial evaluating the superiority of personalised PT compared with BA. The primary endpoint is the difference in depression severity at week 6 between personalised PT and BA, measured using the Montgomery–Åsberg Depression Rating Scale.

This trial has the potential to modify existing clinical guidelines on the treatment of TRD thereby optimizing routine clinical care. In addition, POSIDONIA aims to establish a adaptive trial infrastructure across all six sites of the German Center for Mental Health (DZPG) that can be expanded for iterative optimisation of psychotherapeutic interventions.
