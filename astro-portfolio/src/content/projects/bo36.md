## Overview

BO36 is a longitudinal follow-up study examining the long-term outcomes of psychotherapy across the lifespan, with a unique focus on intergenerational dynamics and how psychotherapy effects may transmit across family generations.

## Study Populations

- Children and adolescents treated in routine care and RCT settings
- Adults with anxiety and mood disorders
- Family units tracked across multiple generations

## Key Questions

- How sustainable are psychotherapy effects over 10+ years?
- Do improvements in parents' mental health confer benefits to their children?
- What biopsychosocial mechanisms contribute to lasting change?

## Methods

- Longitudinal cohort design with annual follow-up assessments
- Data from both randomized controlled trials and routine care settings
- Mixed-methods approach combining quantitative outcomes with qualitative process data
- Part of the DZPG psychotherapy research infrastructure
